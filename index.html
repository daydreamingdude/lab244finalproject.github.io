What is the Coordinated Care Organization Coverage in Your County?
The Oregon Health Plan (OHP) provides health care coverage for Oregonians from all walks of life. This includes working families, children, pregnant women, single adults and seniors. Apply for OHP today. Already have OHP? Check out the OHP handbook to learn about how to use your benefits. Interested in public charge? You can read more about the federal rule.

A coordinated care organization (CCO) is a network of all types of health care providers (physical health care, addictions and mental health care and dental care providers) who work together in their local communities to serve people who receive health care coverage under the Oregon Health Plan (Medicaid). CCOs focus on prevention and helping people manage chronic conditions, like diabetes. This helps reduce unnecessary emergency room visits and gives people support to be healthy.
